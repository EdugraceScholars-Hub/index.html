About

Linguist, Editor & Language Educator

I am a linguistics professional based in Ibadan, Nigeria, specialising in Yorùbá phonology and phonetics, the phonetics–phonology interface, ATR vowel harmony, and tone systems. My research is grounded in rigorous fieldwork and a commitment to the documentation and analysis of African languages.

As an editor at Kharis Publishing (Chicago, USA), I work on academic and general titles, bringing precision and clarity to complex manuscripts. I hold an M.A. in Linguistics from the University of Ibadan and a First Class B.A. from Adekunle Ajasin University, where I graduated as the best student in my faculty and department.

Through EduGrace Scholars Hub, I provide tutoring, phonetic transcription, translation, and academic writing support to students and researchers across multiple disciplines.

Research

Publications & Scholarship

Forthcoming · Brill

"There is No /n/ in Standard Yorùbá"

This article argues that [n] is not an independent phoneme in Standard Yorùbá but functions as a simultaneous allophone of /l/ and /m/ — conditioned by the oral–nasal vowel distinction. A phenomenon the author terms "partial overlapping." Under preparation for publication with Brill.
